Crosswords

Here I will discuss crosswords1, sometimes shortened to Xword, which is something that I have been absolutely obsessed with recently.

Themes

Crosswords can be either themed or themeless. Themed crosswords have a few words throughout them that follow a certain pattern, usually wordplay that the solver will have to figure out as they complete the puzzle. This adds another layer to the crossword solving experience, as in addition to figuring out the clues and filling in the grid, there is an additional puzzle within the crossword.

Themes should be fresh, interesting, narrowly defined and consistently applied throughout the puzzle. If the theme includes a particular kind of pun, for example, then all the puns should be of that kind. Themes and theme entries should be accessible to everyone.

- Excerpt from How to Make a Crossword Puzzle from the New York Times.

Themeless crosswords, by contrast, focus purely on filling out a grid. Because there is no added challenge from a theme, themeless crosswords tend to be more difficult. That is, there are usually fewer words, but they are longer. Rarer and more esoteric terms fill these crosswords, and the clues are tougher.

Types of Themes

There are an infinite amount of themes for crosswords. However, there are a few common types of themes that you will often see the more you complete crosswords.

Ends With...

One common theme is four sets of phrases that all end with a common term. These terms are usually all sinonims, and also usually unrelated to the denotation of the theme entry. These are a bit easier to understand with an example.

Example

Can you figure out the theme of these four entries?

  • PUZZLE PIECE
  • TENURE TRACK
  • PRIME NUMBER
  • CALL NUMBER
  • MACBOOK AIR

Our entries are PUZZLE PIECE, TENURE TRACK, CALL NUMBER, and MACBOOK AIR. We know that a piece is a written, musical, or artistic creation or composition, a track is a recording of one song or piece of music, a number is a song, dance, piece of music, etc., especially one of several in a performance, and an air can be defined as a tune or short melodious composition, typically a song. So our theme is terms that end in synonyms for song.

So piece is parsed as a portion of an object a song. Furthermore, PUZZLE PIECE could be reparsed (punnily) as a piece of music played while solving a puzzle.

In case you were wondering, song in Chinese is 歌曲 (gē qǔ) and 歌 (うた) in Japanese. In Farsi, it is آهنگ.

Other Theme Types

There's tons of other theme types too. Sometimes you'll see words appear BACKWARDS, or you'll see non-letter characters, like H2O (with numbers) or multiple letters in one square. This is called a rebus, and you can input these on NYT crosswords with esc. Rebuses allow for even longer words, so you can have things like supercalifragilisticexpialidocious. Sometimes there's characters like + or - for math-themed crosswords! Try to do some calculations yourself:

Crossword Construction

Making your own crossword can be super duper fun. There are all sorts of tools that help with making crosswords, like word databases and regular expression searching tools. Sometimes constructors even write code to help. Here's an example: 

                
from collections import Counter

# put words and location here
list = "INHEELS UNSEALS UNVEILS"
loc = 4

words = list.split()
counts = Counter()

for word in words:
    counts[word[loc]] += 1

for item, count in counts.items():
    print(item, count)

print()
print("".join(counts.keys()))

This code finds all the possible letters in a certain location for a list of words! Here, list is all the words separated by spaces, and loc is the position to check. The output would look something like:

E 1
A 1
I 1
